Andjela Mladenovic

PhD student at Mila and the Université de Montréal.

Research

Micro

I am interested in how preferences and behavioral patterns are formed over time, and whether simulation-based approaches could help us better understand these processes.

Macro

I am interested in how to design markets and multi-agent environments in which cooperation leads to socially beneficial outcomes.

I am particularly interested in connecting individual-level adaptation with system-level dynamics in settings where traditional assumptions may no longer hold.

Background

Prior to coming to Canada, I spent the first 18 years of my life in Belgrade, Serbia, where I attended the Mathematical Grammar School and competed in mathematics competitions. I later moved to Canada, where I completed my undergraduate studies in engineering at the University of Toronto.

My current research focuses on socially relevant problems, though I still enjoy thinking about open problems in combinatorics and online algorithms.
